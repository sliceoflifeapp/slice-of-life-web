While you're talking, Slice reads your b-roll and places cutaways where they fit — matching the subject of what you're saying to the shot on screen. The result looks like you spent an hour on it.

The output
By default, yes — captions are burned directly into the video so they show on every platform without uploading a separate file. You can toggle them off in the configure screen if you prefer. An SRT sidecar also exports alongside when captions are on, so you can upload it to YouTube or use it in your editor.
The app scores every clip for quality — brightness, sharpness, and clarity of speech. Clips that are too dark, blurry, or very short get filtered automatically. Use the re-edit panel after your first render to adjust.
Technical
Your video files never leave your Mac. Transcription and video assembly run entirely on your device. A small number of image frames (not your full video) are sent to Anthropic's AI for clip analysis and b-roll matching — the same way a photo editing app might send a thumbnail for analysis. Frames are not stored or used for training.
A typical daily vlog renders in 3–8 minutes on Apple Silicon. 4K is slower than 1080p. Re-edits skip the AI analysis step so they're faster, but still take a few minutes to encode.
