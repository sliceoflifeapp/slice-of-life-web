1.1  ·  Mission & Vision
1.1   Mission & Vision
Why
Because the best days of your
life deserve more than a hard drive.

Most people have thousands of hours of footage they've never watched. Not because they don't care — because the gap between filming and finishing a video feels impossibly wide. Slice of Life closes that gap. Drop in your clips, pick your length, and walk away with something worth watching.

Mission

To turn everyday footage into something worth watching — automatically, privately, and personally.

Vision

To become the default way people preserve the moments that matter.

The Idea in One Line

"Document your day. Watch it back."

Target User

iPhone users, casual videographers, journalers, and memory-keepers who film often but never finish the edit.

Platform

Mac app (Apple Silicon & Intel). One-time purchase. No subscription. Everything runs on-device.

1.2  ·  Brand Values
1.2   Brand Values

Four principles that inform every product decision, every line of copy, and every pixel of interface.

Effortless
The effort should be in the living, not the editing. If a user has to think too hard, we've failed. Every interaction should feel like the obvious next step.
Private
Your footage never leaves your machine. In a world where every app wants your data, we protect it by design — not as a feature, but as a foundation.
Personal
Every Slice reflects how you filmed, not what an algorithm wants. The app learns your rhythm, your pacing, your style — and gets better every time.
Lasting
We exist to make something permanent out of something fleeting. The footage you take today will mean more in ten years than it does right now. We hold that seriously.

1.5  ·  Do's & Don'ts
1.5   Do's & Don'ts

Do

Lead with the feeling before the feature. "Your day, remembered" before "AI-powered editing."

Use personal pronouns — "your footage," "your day," "your story." Make it about them, not the app.

Keep sentences short and declarative. One idea per sentence.

Mention privacy often. It's a genuine differentiator and a real concern for our users.

Reference specific moments — a trip, an ordinary Tuesday, a kid's birthday. Specificity creates feeling.

Say "footage," "clips," "memories." These are the words our users use.

Write in American English. "Analyzing," not "Analysing."

Don't

Don't lead with "AI-powered." Let the result speak first; the technology is secondary.

Don't name Whisper or Claude in user-facing copy. Describe what they do, not what they are.

Don't say "content." Say "footage," "clips," or "memories."

Don't use startup jargon — "leverage," "seamless," "robust," "solution."

Don't over-explain the technology. Users don't need to know how it works to appreciate that it works.

Don't use exclamation points in product copy. Confidence doesn't shout.

Don't say "Premier Pro" — say "your editor" or "XML file."

2.1  ·  Logo Usage
2.1   Logo Usage
Slice of Life
Primary — Icon + Wordmark
Icon only — App icon, favicon, avatars
Slice of Life
Compact — Nav, headers, footers
Clear Space

Always maintain clear space equal to the icon height on all four sides. Never crowd the mark with other elements, borders, or competing imagery.

Backgrounds

The white icon is designed for dark surfaces only (#020C18–#0A1628). Never place on white, light, or non-brand-color backgrounds.

File Formats

PNG — App icon, presentations, social media

SVG — Website, scalable use, print-ready exports

PDF — Formal documents, brand materials

Minimum Size

Digital: 16 × 16px (icon only) / 80px wide (lockup)

Print: 0.25in (icon only) / 1in wide (lockup)

Below minimum, use the icon-only version

2.2  ·  Logo Don'ts
2.2   Logo Don'ts

These usages dilute the mark and undermine brand recognition. None are approved.

× Light background
× Stretched / distorted
× Low opacity
× Rotated
× Non-brand background
× Recolored
× Effects / shadows
× Below minimum size
Clear Space Rule

Always maintain a minimum clear zone equal to the height of the icon on all four sides of the mark. No text, borders, competing imagery, or decorative elements should enter this zone. When in doubt, give the logo more room.

Background Guidance

The white icon is designed exclusively for dark brand surfaces — #020C18 through #0A1628. If a background is ambiguous in darkness, default to the full lockup (icon + wordmark) to preserve legibility. Never apply the icon to white, light, or non-brand-color surfaces.
